Chapter One · Governance

How many Pacific Island Countries and Territories (PICTs) do you think came together for our key governance meetings this year?

Core Achievement 1:
Strengthening Regional Governance

What exactly was achieved?

PPHSN Governance Review and Renewal.

  • PPHSN Strategic framework 2026–2035 development.
  • EpiNet strengthening and ToR update.
  • First time convening a One Health TWG (POSW) with both animal & human health.
  • Combined PacMOSSI/PVN 2026 Annual Meetings (9–11 June 2026).
  • 28th PPHSN-CB Meeting Convened — 26–27 March 2026.

Governance Metrics & Impact

  • 18 PICTs and partners brought together across 4 meetings.
  • Endorsement of the Regional EpiNet (REpiNet) Concept.
  • PPHSN and its 7 service networks are fully operational, shifting from national-focus to a regional response capacity.
  • Expanding PPHSN member partnership.
  • Consultation and preparation for the celebration of the 30th anniversary of PPHSN.
Chapter Two · Capacity

Which critical field epidemiology training modules were successfully delivered for the Cook Islands and French Polynesia?

Core Achievement 2:
Epidemiology, Surveillance & RCCE Capacity

Adaptive & Responsive Approaches

  • Cook Islands & French Polynesia PGCFE Module 5 (final) delivered.
  • Tonga epidemiology and surveillance tailor-made training delivered.
  • PGDAE training delivered (regional cohort initiated).
  • RCCE tailor-made training for USAPIs delivered by PIHOA and SPC.

Key Metrics

  • French Polynesia PGCFE: Module 4 (16 participants), Module 5 (15 participants).
  • Cook Islands PGCFE: Module 5 (13 participants).
  • Regional PGDAE: 14 participants from 8 PICTs.
  • RCCE: 16 participants from 5 USAPIs.

Broader Impact: Ability to detect, assess and respond to Public Health threats strengthened.

Chapter Three · Early Warning

What major climate-sensitive disease did we prototype an interactive early warning dashboard for this year?

Core Achievement 3:
Strengthening Early Warning Systems

What was achieved?

Integrating climate information into predictive models.

  • Development of the Leptospirosis Dashboard (prototype for surveillance and early warning).
  • Active in Fiji, New Caledonia, Wallis and Futuna, and French Polynesia.
  • Displays climate and leptospirosis cases with predictions for the next 3 months via gauge visualisation.

Broader Organizational Impact

  • Supports preparedness and response to outbreaks.
  • Showcases the power of shared surveillance data.
  • Potential replication for other climate-sensitive diseases.
